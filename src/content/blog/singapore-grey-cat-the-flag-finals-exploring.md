## foreword

i participate in competitions called CTFs (capture the flag). as wikipedia states, participants attempt to find text strings, called "flags", which are secretly hidden in purposefully vulnerable programs or websites.

i mainly focus on the category of cryptography: analyzing encrypted or encoded data and identifying weaknesses in cryptographic algorithms. challenges typically fall under a few primary domains, but typically the more interesting ones are all modern cryptography. these challenges test your understanding of mathematical algorithms and how they are coded in the real world. however, sometimes CTF organizers can invent completely new cipher ideas and mix them with mathematical algorithms, which i feel is the epitome of modern cryptography.

one of the most interesting aspects of CTF are onsite finals. many CTFs are a one time experience, typically on the weekend, which we will define as friday to sunday. however, some act as a qualifier for a finals event held later on. notable examples include DEFCON and DiceCTF. these events are more memorable to players, especially as most interactions with your teammates are online, and you rarely get to see your friends in person. these events are becoming more and more rare, especially with the development of artificial intelligence.

the last precursor is about ai. as ottersec beautifully puts it:

> “You spend months working on a novel challenge, and a new model comes out a day before the CTF and one-shots the challenge. The current progress in AI development has led to CTFs becoming pay-to-win.”

because of this, many players have lost incentive to compete, and have quit and moved on with their lives. while there may not be a definitive solution to this problem yet, there have been many novel ideas recently which have held up. one idea, is just to ban ai. while this can never be 100% effective, is pretty fun to have for an onsite final. it brings back the old joy that people experienced, communicating with teammates and solving challenges manually.

## day -1

this was actually the day of my high school graduation. i’ve officially graduated from the academy for medical science technology at the bergen county academies. this also means i’m off to college now.

typically, after graduation, there is a program called Project Graduation that high schools offer. it is an all-night post-graduation celebration, often held in a “mystery” location and promised to be a fun farewell to all your classmates.

honestly, it wasn’t too bad. we were taken to american dream. on the bus ride there, a couple friends and i watched the south korea game, which ultimately disappointed all of them as south korea lost. arriving there, all the fun and joy was killed. we spent 30 minutes getting into a group, just for us all to go to beast burger. while mr beast might be a good guy in life, that food was actually horrendous. i waited for quite a long time only to receive a very disappointing burger and fries such that my appetite was completely lost. however the milkshake was pretty decent.

after that, we got escorted to a trampoline park. there was basketball, a bunch of slides, and a lot of miscellaneous trampolines. notable highlights include pushing my friend into a foam pit and doing a laser maze which my partner and i horribly failed. the only problem is that we genuinely spent way too long here, which we could have used to spend more time in … the nickelodeon universe theme park. we had ~45 minutes here. didn’t help that half the rides were closed. i went on some swing ride, rode shredder twice, and narrowly avoided having to ride any ride that was upside down. my reasoning was that i had some cuts on my stomach from being impaled by a fence the day earlier, but also i genuinely was not trying to get on the 360 degree ride. my friend really didn’t like that one (sorry!)

as we returned back to the school for pickup, i could only think to myself about my high school experience. it was pretty unique in many ways, yet i dont know if i would do it again. at this time it was 330 am.

## day 0

technically i can merge day -1 and 0, but for the sake of simplicity lets just go with this. its 330 am, and i have a flight to catch at 6am.

fortunately, i didn’t drive to the airport, because why would i ever drive past 11!! i got dropped off, and it was 4am. the airport was pretty idyllic. there weren't any busy families rushing, and having a nice quiet empty airport with the dark skies was something i really enjoyed. security and all didn’t take long, and i had 2 hours to myself at the gate.

now with this free time, i debated on going to the lounge, but ultimately decided against it as it was pretty useless anyways. however, i was starving and it didnt help that most of these restaurants were closed. i spent the time talking with the same friend, helping her experience her first ever all nighter!!! she also had never tried monster, so at project graduation i got her one. this probably was a main reason why she was awake all night. unfortunately, i didn’t get to experience the sunrise live with her, so instead we saw it through our respective windows. her out her bedroom, and me out the airplane gate.

it was now time to board. after brief goodbyes, i entered the plane, and immediately passed out. actually, i don’t fully recall what happened, but i do know that i didn’t activate free messaging. i also vaguely remember asking for water a couple of times. the one thing about flying aisle is that you don’t have to deal with having to ask people whenever you want to get up, so i think at one point i got up and just walked around.

at this point i realized my grave mistake, so i quickly enabled messaging and then spent the rest of the flight yapping away while also counting the number of sleeping people in a 5 row radius of me. i also convinced the flight attendant to give me 20 bags of pretzels.

after landing in sfo, i spent about 20 minutes walking to the gate. to my disappointment, not a single restaurant was actually open near the gate, which meant i had to deal with my hunger again. alas, i spent the time calling the same friend and aimlessly staring out the window while yapping. i think we discussed a lot about instagram posts or something, and also just reminiscing high school, even tho it genuinely had been less than 12 hours since we graduated.

## day 1

somehow i was awake and not awake at the same time, because i dont really remember what happened except i wasn’t asleep. this was partially due to the fact that my roommate was supposed to arrive at 3 am so i had an obligation to wait for him. i spent most of the 3 hours waiting texting my friend, and after my roommate arrived, decided it was a great time for a night walk while being on call.

so from 3 to 5 am, instead of sleeping, i walked around the streets of singapore without my shoes or my glasses. i was just on call yapping away and enjoying life for the moment. do i regret my actions? no. being on call is so rare that it was truly really memorable and made me feel happy.

at 5 am, i decided that sleeping was a pretty good idea, given i would probably need to allnighter for the competition anyways the next night. this ended up not being true. but alas, it was time to rest.

i woke up at 730, showered and got ready for the competition. i met my teammates in the lobby at 815, and we got on the bus. the only 2 “americans” ended up being my teammate and i, and apart from the 3 europeans, everyone else was “asian” (by nationality).

getting there, the CTF was delayed by about an hour, so we spent that time getting merch and setting up. somehow, yours truly forgot to fill out the merch form, but it ended up not mattering as they had plenty of extras. they gave us this really tough windbreaker type jacket and a bag with plushies, a shirt, and some other extras.

we also were introduced to the convenience store, where we had NO limit and the only rule was to not go crazy and be a normal human being. i ended up having a pretty light breakfast, certainly not ordinary:

i socialized with some of the koreans, who shared their great hatred for full slopping, and knew someone on my team who wasn’t here.

and then it was time for the CTF to begin. immediately, i noticed there was actually a developed crypto category. no misc or osint, but there was an ai category as well. i started off with this category, looking at a rubiks cube challenge, where you had to solve 100 cubes in a row. i spent about 30 minutes trying to set it up, but didn’t have much progress after that, as i didn’t really understand what was going on and i kept running into issues.

by this time my teammate was working on the first crypto challenge, and had a full solve, except the implementation was slightly messed up. with ai it could have taken like 2 seconds to fix, but we spent about 30 minutes realizing the mistakes and quickly tidied that up for a nice flag. i also came to the grand realization that the crypto in this CTF would be solvable, unlike LAKE, where I was 6 minutes late.

i then proceeded to work on the second crypto, `half bit wonder`. however, i got distracted by a 30 page pdf for the final crypto, `wintertime`. I spent about an hour reading the pdf, only to realize i probably wouldn’t understand most of it after being stuck on the first 15 pages. i went back to half bit, and figured out the solve pretty quickly. it didn’t take much time after to implement, and soon , at like 1pm, i had my flag. the flag paid tribute to daniel bleichenbacher, notable for attacks against the RSA cryptosystem. technically, i wasn’t first blood, but some team’s crypto solves got removed, so posthumously this was a first blood.

at this time it was lunch. if im being honest, i decided to try some local food option, and i ate a grand total of two bites before i realized this was NOT for me. as such, i resorted to eating more apples and a bag of coca-cola gummies. at this point, i also failed locking in, as my stomach hurt too much. i decided to play some brawl stars, but the wifi was really laggy, so i ended up returning to work on crypto.

the third crypto was called `random brainrot`, and yes, it did involve six and seven. the difficulty gap from the first 2 crypto to the remaining 3 was huge. part of this was just me being bad, but also i was starting to feel the tiredness. i popped a white monster and kept going away at this challenge. my teammate had a decent amount of progress but there were no clear paths in his work, so i was more confused than understanding what was happening. fortunately, he linked a couple papers, so i spent some time reading these.

my teammate kept working on this challenge, and i spent more time reading up on this. at the same time, there were some onsite challenges, so i was pretty curious. unfortunately, they were all technical, with hardware, which i knew nothing about. however, there was this challenge called `hidden in plain sight` which alluded to a flag being hidden on our badges. my teammate and i assumed it was on the participant badges, but it was apparently on the hardware badges.

so essentially, there was morse code on our badges, and we thought we had decrypted the flag. turns out, the organizers themselves didn't even realize the morse code decrypted to something, and we rabbit holed for 30 minutes! dont we love CTF!!

i then returned to go back to the crypto. i started to gain an understanding but was fighting sleep to my fullest. because of this, i decided to take shots of milkis, as i got a 5 pack earlier in the morning. i tried grape, apple, and normal milkis. i would have to rate grape the best, followed by normal, and then apple. this helped my concentration, and i proceeded to lock in whilst listening to yoasobi until “dinner”.

one thing that’s pretty abnormal is my eat and sleep schedule. typically, i eat dinner at 10pm, or even later. but here, dinner was served around 6pm. i was also really hungry and tired, so i decided to eat up a bit early. this time, i just took what seemed to be the most edible. there was indian, but i decided against it. i had grilled teriyaki chicken, some egg noodles, and some seafood. this was promptly washed down with a lot of water and one milkis. i returned to my desk, only to get hit with a wave of exhaustion. i literally couldn't keep my eyes open for greater than 5 minutes. at last, i opened a ticket, and asked to use the sleeping room. the organizers had prepared a couple inflatable mattresses. thankfully, no one else was tired yet, so i had the room to myself, which meant ultimate quiet and no weird smells. at this point in time, my roommate had been sleeping at the table for a couple hours now.

during my sleep, i found the mattresses to be really comfortable. i wish there was a blanket as well. i ended up sleeping for only 2.5 hours, though it felt like much more. but more importantly, i had figured out the idea to `random brainrot`. i went back to the desk, to see my other teammates working on the hardware challenge with music, and the other still attempting a bit of forensics. i wanted to help, but i just got redirected to grinding crypto. i wasted a hour walking around and going outside, as the air inside was really stuffy. there i sat down and thought about this challenge, but also spent some time texting my friends, as i was getting kind of bored.

i opened the fourth crypto, and it genuinely looked like a foreign language. it was relating to native american braids. i wrote down some of my initial thoughts, after reading up a bit on the terminology. while i was doing so, i figured out two things. one, what i was doing for this challenge, but also, what i was doing for `random brainrot`. i ran back up the stairs and told my teammate the full idea, and said i would finish it up.

the idea took about 20 minutes to write up, and then a hour and a half to implement. here i really wished i had ai. i would have been long done with this challenge. instead, i had to learn how important indenting was and how not easy it was to troubleshoot. after going through all the errors, i ended up getting the LOCAL flag at 1am. for those non technical, you can solve a challenge locally, and sometimes are provided remote instances, where you can connect and do the same program remotely to get the flag used for the competition. however, i had a slight error. we had to connect to openvpn to access stuff, which i was having a bit of trouble doing. after fixing all of that, i got the flag. unfortunately, i was nowhere near first blood, because the koreans and some other team figured this out really quickly. in hindsight, it was pretty simple if you didn’t rabbit hole.

## day 2

at 12, i could see that all my teammates were tired, as well as most of the other competitors. i guess this is where the america “jetlag” came in good use, because i was completely wide awake. at this point, the organizers hosted a flappy bird competition where you had to do six seven with your hands in order to move the bird. it was pretty amusing to watch a lot of other people fail after 1. i wanted to play myself, but i knew it would just distract me more.

at this time, the organizers announced the greatest thing possible. we could order mcdonalds. AND they would pay for it. so i got the usual chicken nuggets. i tried getting a mcflurry, but the machine was broken. while i waited a hour for the delivery to arrive, i went back to working on the braids challenge, titled `Long Khipu Braids`. i figured out the full theoretical solution across the next 30 minutes, and thought i was in a good place to take a bit of a break. i talked to the organizer, who happened to be the challenge creator. i didn’t know that, but he asked what i was working on so i showed him my work and said i didnt know if what i was doing was accurate. he just laughed and told me to try it.

an hour later, my nuggets arrived, and after annoying the organizers atleast 5 times about them, i was very relieved to be eating them. i also opened a ticket asking if the progress was right, and that’s when i found out the guy i was talking to earlier was actually the challenge author, who told me to just try it. this meant i had the right idea, and now we had to implement!!

that was much more of a nightmare than the other one. i actually had to learn everything from the beginning, which took a LOT of time. during my arduous struggle, my teammates managed to put up good points on the board, by solving KOTH challenges and their precursors. KOTH challenges are live dynamic challenges where the first place solution gets 1k points, and all other solutions are relative to that and scored accordingly. with these challenges, we were comfortably in the top 5, and it was a battle to stay in the top 3, as we would hover around third or fourth.

at this time, it was 5am, and i was feeling quite good. almost everyone else was asleep, including the organizers. i went back outside to get some fresh air, and i heard birds chirping. to be honest, before june, i hadn’t really heard birds chirping in the early morning, partly due to my horrendous sleep schedule.

i continued writing the script, taking occasional breaks because my eyes hurt a bit and also because i kept getting distracted (scrolling reels). at 7am, the store reopened, and i was very desperate for food.

i got more apples, watermelon, mentos, and coca-cola candy. this was sufficient for me to get a bit of energy, and i noticed other people slowly waking up. after eating such a crude breakfast, i surprisingly was able to figure out all the errors in the code and solved the challenge within a hour. at 8am sharp, i submitted the flag, and got my first blood! to celebrate, i drank a peach monster lol. it tasted very mediocre. i then talked with the author a bit behind methodology of solving the challenge and my ideas regarding it. i really thought it was a novel idea and i actually enjoyed it.

by now, the scoreboard was frozen, and pre freeze we were fourth. this was good enough to put us in top 5, but i wanted to be a bit higher. however, i knew i was NOT solving that last crypto, which left me with looking at the ai category. there was a neural network challenge which seemed really interesting, and so i started to look at that.

while i was doing that, one of my teammates, the one who was working on `random brainrot`, managed to get a first blood on a web challenge. this was actually really impressive, given the only other web challenge we solved was a beginner one which still took us a long amount of time. i was pretty confident that this would put us in third place. my teammate then left, leaving me to be the only person left at the venue representing my team.

sitting down, i just worked on the challenge, learning a lot more than actually coming up with a solve. i also cleaned up the desk a bit and talked with one of the organizers about life. apparently, we couldn't book the hotel right next to the venue as it required everyone being 18+. there was only one exception to that, yours truly.

i ended up coming up with the solve 5 minutes before the competition ended, which meant no time to implement. it was pretty sad, but it was also the most fun CTF i have played in a really long time. there was no bus back to the hotel, so i was relying on taking public transportation.

except the bus didn’t show up, and my uber wouldn't work because i was 17. this meant i had to walk 3 miles in the scorching hot sun back to the hotel. i was texting my friend and walking on the street, and almost walked into a pole twice. i also managed to play a bit of cricket with locals. unfortunately, they didn’t want to take a picture with me.

i reached the hotel and immediately lied down on the bed for a while, before deciding to shower and planning out the rest of my singapore trip. my roommate had to leave due to exams, so after seeing him off, i decided to plan my trip.

this day i planned to shop a bit, so i wanted to go to holland village, imm, jemm mall, and leave more for tomorrow. there was also the awards ceremony at 6, so i didn’t want to be too far from the hotel so i could attend.

at holland village, i walked around a bit and enjoyed the scenery. i ended up buying a miffy keychain for my friend and wanted to go try local food. i stood in line for 30 minutes, just to find out they don’t take alipay or credit card, so my time was wasted. i ended up settling for this noodle place nearby, which tasted amazing considering how famished i was.

## day 3

i woke up finding out that the prize for winning was ramen. no, not a ramen shaped trophy, literal ramen. unfortunately my teammates who had my trophy were asleep and were going to leave while i was out, so i told them to leave it at the front desk.

at about 930 i checked out, left my bags, and embarked on some breakfast. most places weren’t open, so i just had some mexican breakfast burrito along with jarritos. by now i had mastered the train system, and in 40 minutes i found myself at the iconic bugis street right around opening.

the next 3 hours blew by pretty quickly. i explored the second and third floors, and ended up getting a couple of shirts. notably, the third floor had a couple of chinese shirts. my minnesotan friend couldn’t translate the chinese at all, and my chatgpt uploads had to do the rest. unfortunately, the ocr was pretty bad, so i ended up asking some random chinese woman for assistance. she even helped me bargain a bit which was pretty nice.

i went outside to the surrounding markets and spent some time trying to get some cash. it took about 30 minutes to find a local cash exchange place, in which i exchanged 30 dollars from the school grad gift to spend. i ended up using this to get some clothes and a drink. i had about 8 sgd left by the end of it. importantly, i found a gengar shirt!! as much as i like gengar, i dont have ANYTHING related to him.

the next location on my schedule was haji lane, which was a single lane filled with intricate artwork and vintage stores. it was also really hot outside, so i walked into every single store i could to save myself from the heat. at this time, my phone was about to die, so i used the macbook charging method while i shopped around. i didn’t end up getting much, just a blind box tshirt for one of the guys i called earlier and a couple smiski stickers.

## day 4

technically by travel times this should be a new day.

i arrived at munich at like 5am, which meant everything was closed. i went to the lounge, showered but skipped eating. i was already kind of full from the flight. the security check for the gate was closed, so i killed 2 hours on the travelator doing as many laps as i could. eventually i sat down at another gate and just did work that i had been slacking off on.

my luck was astronomical, so i had the row to myself again. i didn’t waste anytime sleeping, and i even missed the first meal which was kind of unfortunate. i was awake for like 3 hours max on this flight, and i spent it skimming through more harry potter and playing chess and mahjong with the computers. my birthday was also the next day, so i thought about what i wanted to do before landing.

## conclusion

apologies if this isn’t super formal. i mainly intended to just write my thoughts down about the experience and stuff i liked. im pretty grateful to the team for letting me go to this competition, as i wouldnt have had this interesting experience without them. im also grateful to the people i could talk to on this trip, or else i would have been really bored half the time. this also ended up being a lot longer than usual, but hopefully it was a captivating read. if not, sorry for wasting your time..
